**Document Title:** Scholarship Opportunities and Eligibility
**Document Number:** FIN005
**Effective Date:** 2023-09-01

## 1. Overview and Purpose

This document provides comprehensive information about the various scholarship opportunities available to students at Westfield State University. The purpose is to outline the eligibility criteria, application procedures, and renewal requirements for merit-based, need-based, departmental, and external scholarships. Our goal is to help students identify and apply for financial assistance to support their academic pursuits.

## 2. Scholarship Categories

Westfield State University offers a wide range of scholarships to recognize academic achievement, assist with financial need, and support students in specific fields of study.

### 2.1. Merit-Based Scholarships

Merit-based scholarships are awarded to students who have demonstrated exceptional academic performance. These scholarships are competitive and are based on a holistic review of the student's application for admission.

| Scholarship Name | Award Amount (per year) | Eligibility Criteria |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Presidential Scholarship | $10,000 | Minimum 3.9 GPA, top 5% of graduating class, demonstrated leadership |
| Provost's Scholarship | $7,500 | Minimum 3.75 GPA, top 10% of graduating class |
| Dean's Scholarship | $5,000 | Minimum 3.5 GPA, top 15% of graduating class |

### 2.2. Need-Based Scholarships

Need-based scholarships are available to students who demonstrate financial need as determined by the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA). Students must complete the FAFSA to be considered for these awards.

| Scholarship Name | Award Amount (per year) | Eligibility Criteria |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Westfield Access Grant | Up to $8,000 | Significant demonstrated financial need based on FAFSA results |
| Opportunity Grant | Up to $4,000 | Demonstrated financial need and strong academic potential |

### 2.3. Departmental Scholarships

Various academic departments at Westfield State University offer scholarships to students majoring in their respective fields. These scholarships are funded by alumni, donors, and the university to support students in their chosen disciplines.

| Department | Scholarship Example | Award Amount (per year) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| College of Business | Johnson Future Business Leaders Scholarship | $3,000 |
| College of Engineering | STEM Innovators Scholarship | $5,000 |
| College of Arts & Sciences | Creative Arts Fellowship | $2,500 |

### 2.4. External Scholarships

Students are encouraged to seek out and apply for external scholarships from various organizations, foundations, and corporations. The Student Financial Services Office maintains a database of external scholarship opportunities on the university website.

## 3. Application Procedures

### 3.1. University Scholarships

1.  **Apply for Admission:** All applicants for admission to Westfield State University are automatically considered for merit-based scholarships.
2.  **Complete the FAFSA:** To be considered for need-based scholarships, students must complete the FAFSA by the priority deadline.
3.  **Departmental Applications:** Students should contact their academic department for information on departmental scholarship applications and deadlines.

### 3.2. External Scholarships

Students are responsible for researching and applying for external scholarships. Deadlines and application requirements vary by organization.

## 4. Important Deadlines

-   **Priority FAFSA Deadline:** March 1
-   **Admission Application Deadline for Scholarship Consideration:** January 15
-   **Departmental Scholarship Deadlines:** Vary by department (typically February 1 - April 1)

## 5. Renewal Requirements

Most university scholarships are renewable for up to four years, provided the student meets the renewal criteria:

-   Maintain a minimum cumulative GPA (typically 3.0 for merit scholarships).
-   Maintain full-time enrollment status.
-   Meet any additional requirements specific to the scholarship.

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Can I receive more than one scholarship?**
A: Yes, students may receive multiple scholarships, but the total amount of financial aid cannot exceed the cost of attendance.

**Q: Are international students eligible for scholarships?**
A: Yes, international students are eligible for merit-based and departmental scholarships. They are not eligible for need-based aid from the federal government but may be eligible for institutional need-based aid.

**Q: What happens if my GPA falls below the renewal requirement?**
A: Students who do not meet the renewal requirements for their scholarship will be notified by the Student Financial Services Office. An appeal process is available for students with extenuating circumstances.
